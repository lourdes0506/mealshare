ABOUT US

My friend Y. Jeffery Godson and I, as fresh graduates, have decided to dedicate our efforts toward addressing one of the most pressing yet often overlooked social issues ‚Äî the wastage of food and the simultaneous rise in hunger among millions. Every day, vast quantities of edible food are wasted in weddings, parties, hotels, restaurants, and large-scale food industries. According to the Food and Agriculture Organization (FAO), roughly one-third of all food produced globally ‚Äî approximately 1.3 billion tonnes ‚Äî is wasted each year, even though much of it is still perfectly consumable. In India alone, it is estimated that 40% of the food produced is wasted annually, especially during post-harvest, processing, and retail stages, as well as from consumer habits and event catering. Meanwhile, over 7,000 people die of hunger every single day worldwide, and one in every nine people globally (more than 820 million people) goes to bed hungry, despite the world producing more than enough food to feed everyone. In India, hunger remains a grave concern, with the country ranking 111th out of 125 in the 2023 Global Hunger Index. The contradiction is heartbreaking ‚Äî while good food ends up in bins, children suffer from malnutrition, and families are left starving.

To bridge this painful gap, we have developed a digital solution ‚Äî Mealshare, a web application built with the vision of "Where Waste Fuels Hope." Our platform acts as a bridge between food donors, such as restaurants, households, event organizers, and grocery outlets, and NGOs or local volunteers who serve the underprivileged. Through real-time alerts and geo-mapping, our app makes it easy for donors to notify nearby NGOs when surplus food is available. It helps reduce food waste, streamline distribution, and ensure hygienic, timely delivery to those in need. What once ended up in the trash now finds its way to empty plates, turning excess into impact. Mealshare is not just an app; it's a step toward a more responsible, humane, and sustainable world.
